Hungarian Goulash

Desciption:

Though there are many debates about what the true authentic hungarian goulash (written as guly√°s in hungarian) consists of, there is one thing that all variations have in common: a lot of paprika. Considered to be Hungary's national dish, this stew is made of few ingredients, yet it yields a lot of incredible flavor.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons pork lard
  • 680 gr yellow onions
  • 30 grams Hungarian sweet paprika
  • 680 grams stewing beef, cut into ~ 2 x 2 x 2 cm cubes
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 red bell peppers, seeded, membranes removed, cut into ~ 2 cm pieces
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, seeded, membranes removed, cut into ~2 cm pieces
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, cut into ~2 cm chunks
  • 1.2 l beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 0.5 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: 0.5 teaspoon crushed caraway seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a dutch oven or a heavy soup pot, add the pork lard and melt it over medium heat
  2. Add onions and fry until they begin to brown (~ 7-10 minutes)>
  3. Add the stewing beef, cook until it begins to brown (~7-10 minutes)
  4. Add bell peppers, tomatoes, and garlic, cook for another 6-8 minutes.
  5. Remove the pot from the heat and stir in the paprika, salt, pepper and caraway (if using).
    6. Caution! Bell pepper becomes bitter if burnt!
  6. Remove pot from stove, pour in beef broth and add the bay leaf
  7. Return pot to stove and bring to a boil on high heat
  8. Reduce heat to medium, cover the pot and let it simmer for 40 minutes
  9. Add carrots and potatoes, return to boil.
  10. Reduce heat to medium, cover the pot and let it simmer for another 30-40 minutes or until beef is tender
  11. Add salt to taste, remove bay leaf

Tip: serve with sour cream and some slices of white bread
